## Getting Started

### What is M365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot is your AI assistant that helps you find information, answer questions, and complete tasks using natural conversation. Think of it as a knowledgeable colleague who knows all about your business rules and can help you understand them.

### How to Access Copilot

**Option 1: In Microsoft Teams**
1. Open Microsoft Teams
2. Look for the Copilot icon (sparkle icon) in the left sidebar
3. Click on it to start a conversation

**Option 2: In Microsoft Word**
1. Open a Word document
2. Look for the Copilot icon in the toolbar
3. Click "Ask Copilot" to get help

**Option 3: In Microsoft Outlook**
1. Open Outlook
2. Click the Copilot icon when composing an email
3. Ask for help with your message

### Starting Your First Conversation

**Step 1: Open Copilot**
- Click the Copilot icon in your M365 application

**Step 2: Start with a greeting**
- Type: "Hello, I need help with margin calculations"

**Step 3: Ask your question**
- Type your question in plain English
- Example: "What is the introduction overview about?"

**Step 4: Follow up**
- Copilot will respond with information
- Ask follow-up questions for more details

## Understanding Your Skills

### What are Skills?

Skills are like specialized knowledge areas that Copilot has learned about your business. Each Skill covers a specific topic, such as:

- Introduction Overview
- Risk Parameters
- Input Data
- Market Risk
- Margin Adjustment
- And more...

### Available Skills

**For Business Analysts (Type A)**:
- Introduction Overview - Learn about the overall process
- Margin Adjustment - Understand how margins are adjusted
- Corporate Action - Learn about corporate action processing

**For QA Leads (Type B)**:
- Risk Parameters - Review risk parameter specifications
- Other Risk - Understand additional risk components

**For Automation Testers (Type C)**:
- Input Data - Learn about data specifications
- Position Processing - Understand position logic
- Calculation Examples - See calculation examples

### How to Use Skills

**Method 1: Ask Directly**
- Simply ask about a topic
- Example: "Tell me about risk parameters"
- Copilot will use the appropriate Skill

**Method 2: Ask for Specific Information**
- Be specific about what you need
- Example: "What are the requirements for input data?"
- Copilot will provide detailed information

**Method 3: Step-by-Step Guidance**
- Ask for process guidance
- Example: "How do I understand the margin adjustment process?"
- Copilot will guide you step by step

## Working with Business Rules

### Understanding Rule Sources

**What to Ask**:
- "Where does this rule come from?"
- "What document contains this information?"
- "When was this rule last updated?"

### Checking Rule Versions

**What to Ask**:
- "What version of the rules are we using?"
- "Has this rule changed recently?"
- "What is the current rule version?"

### Validating Information

**What to Ask**:
- "Is this information still current?"
- "Has this been verified?"
- "Where can I verify this information?"

### Getting Help with Complex Topics

**Breaking Down Complex Topics**:
1. Start broad: "Tell me about market risk"
2. Get specific: "What are the components of market risk?"
3. Deep dive: "Explain the HVaR calculation"
4. Get examples: "Can you show me a calculation example?"

## Troubleshooting

### When Copilot Doesn't Understand

**If Copilot gives an unclear answer**:
- Try rephrasing your question
- Be more specific
- Use simpler language
- Reference specific rule IDs if you know them

**Example**:
- Instead of: "Tell me about that thing"
- Try: "Tell me about margin adjustment process"

### When Information Seems Outdated

**What to Do**:
1. Ask: "When was this information last updated?"
2. Check the rule version
3. Ask for the source document
4. Verify with the latest documentation

### When You Need Human Help

**Escalation Path**:
1. Try asking Copilot differently
2. Check the usage guidelines document
3. Contact your system administrator
4. Reach out to the business rules team

### Common Questions and Answers

**Q: Why can't Copilot find my topic?**
A: Try using different words or check if the Skill exists. You can ask "What Skills are available?"

**Q: The answer seems incomplete. What should I do?**
A: Ask follow-up questions like "Can you tell me more?" or "What else should I know?"

**Q: How do I know if the information is correct?**
A: Ask for the source: "Where does this information come from?" Copilot will provide rule references.

**Q: Can I use Copilot to update information?**
A: No, Copilot provides information only. To update, contact your system administrator.

## Best Practices

### Do's

✓ **Be Specific**: The more specific your question, the better the answer
- Good: "What are the input data requirements for position processing?"
- Less effective: "Tell me about data"

✓ **Use Natural Language**: Talk to Copilot like you would to a colleague
- Good: "How do I calculate market risk?"
- Less effective: "market risk calculation algorithm"

✓ **Ask Follow-Up Questions**: Dive deeper into topics
- "Can you explain that more?"
- "What happens next?"
- "Why is that important?"

✓ **Reference Rule IDs**: If you know them, use them
- "Explain rule PROC-005"
- "What does DATA-010 mean?"

### Don'ts

✗ **Don't Use Technical Jargon**: Keep it simple
- Avoid: "Execute the HVaR computation module"
- Use: "How is market risk calculated?"

✗ **Don't Ask Multiple Questions at Once**: Break them up
- Avoid: "What is market risk and how is it calculated and what are the requirements?"
- Use: Ask one question at a time

✗ **Don't Expect Copilot to Make Decisions**: It provides information
- Avoid: "Should I approve this margin calculation?"
- Use: "What are the requirements for margin approval?"
